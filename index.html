Visualizing STEAM: a digital game platform

Objective

Our visualization aims to develop the interactive analytics dashboard that builds on the Steam data on games and individual players collected through open-source APIs to offer the insightful look at recent trends in the gaming industry.

We start by visualizating the release rate of the games by year. This chart is interactive, so you can filter the years range.

We conducted a sampling process to curate a dataset comprising the 250 most reviewed games that were released before the year 2019, ensuring that each selected game possesses a minimum age of five years. This criterion not only guarantees a diverse representation of the gaming landscape but also focuses on titles that have withstood the test of time.

Questions

This visualization aims to answer some question about to the gaming culture on Steam:

  1. What are the most popular genres and tags on Steam?
  2. What games are more popular right now?
  3. What is the relation of popularity and reviews?
  4. What is the relation of price and reviews?
  5. What countries are producing more games?

Genres

The pie chart on the left side compares the quantity by year of released games separated by genre [1]. Note that a single game can have multiple genres (e.g. Grand Theft Auto V is both Action and Adventure), in that case, each genre is considered.

The line chart on the right side displays player engagement trends for the different game genres over the years. Each line represents a genre and compares the popularity of a gender in the players community [2].

Tags

On Steam, tags serve as descriptive keywords for the Steam community collectively. Unlike genres, those tags are added by the players and are based on user input and votes.

This freedom of input permits more specific descriptions and offen bring rich information about the game. quantity by year of released games separated by genre.

As an example, we have Grand Theft Auto V with genres equals "Action, Adventure" and tags equals "Open World, Action, Automobile Sim, Multiplayer, Crime', Third Person, ...", with way more details.

We now present a word cloud where we highlight the most frequent tags added by the users. [1]

Most popular games

We now present the top 10 most popular games on Steam, the games are ordered by the mean quantity of playes between the years of 2019 and 2023 [2].

Review and popularity

Steam games reviews are binary, each player can give one positive or negative review. To analyse them, we present a scatter plot with the review rate (positive reviews / total reviews) and popularity (average number of players between jan-2019 and sep-2023) [3].

Price and popularity

One interesting comparation is the proportion of free and paid games [4]. We present this data on the pie chart on the right.

We consider the initial price (price of the game on the release). To analyse the relationship between price and popularity, below we present a scatter plot with the price and popularity (average number of players between jan-2019 and sep-2023) [4].

Developers

We are also interested in the Steam developer community. We first analise the supported plaforms (operational systems) of the games on the pie chart on the right [5]. There are tree possible platforms: Windows, Linux and Mac, and one interesting fact is that all games support Windows Steam.

We also analise relationship between the developers and games using a graph plot. In this plot, red circles represent the games while blue circles represents the developers. Also, the developer nodes are sized by the sum of popularity of all it's games.

Developers World Map

To see what countries are producing more games, we make a world choropleth map, where each country is shaded to show the quantity of game development companies [6].
